---
title: Indian Knowledge Systems
description: Texts on traditional systems of organized knowledge, including science, mathematics, technical disciplines, applied learning, and knowledge classification.
---

<p><a href="/library/historical-methods" class="blank linked">Historical Methods</a>, by Shonaleeka Kaul - Discussion of historical method with relevance to Sanskrit and Indian civilizational sources.</p>
<p><a href="/library/on-the-nature-of-mathematics-and-scientific-knowledge-in-indian-tradition" class="blank linked">On the Nature of Mathematics and Scientific Knowledge in Indian Tradition</a>, by MD Srinivas - Study of how Indian traditions theorized mathematics, knowledge, and scientific reasoning.</p>
<p><a href="/library/indian-science-and-technology-in-the-18th-century" class="blank linked">Indian Science and Technology in the 18th Century</a>, by Dharampal - Documentary study of scientific and technical practices in early modern India.</p>
<p><a href="/library/agnihotra-kranti" class="blank linked">Agnihotra Kranti</a>, by Unknown - Text advocating or explaining the revival of Agnihotra and related Vedic fire ritual practice.</p>
<p><a href="/library/lokopakara" class="blank linked">Lokopakara</a>, by Unknown - Traditional practical compendium on useful arts, household knowledge, and applied sciences.</p>
