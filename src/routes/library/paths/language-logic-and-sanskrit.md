---
title: Language, Logic, and Sanskrit
description: Works on Sanskrit language, grammar, semantics, lexicography, linguistic philosophy, and systems of logical reasoning.
---

<p><a href="/library/logic-language-and-reality" class="blank linked">Logic, Language and Reality</a>, by Bimal Krishna Matilal - Philosophical study connecting logic, linguistic analysis, and theories of reality in Indian thought.</p>
<p><a href="/library/theories-of-language" class="blank linked">Theories of Language</a>, by Korada Subrahmanyam - Study of Indian theories of language, meaning, signification, and verbal cognition.</p>
<p><a href="/library/a-scheme-for-knowledge-representation-in-samskritam" class="blank linked">A Scheme for Knowledge Representation in Samskritam</a>, by P. Ramanujan - Paper exploring Sanskrit-based formal knowledge representation and semantic structuring.</p>
<p><a href="/library/bhartṛhari’s-linguistic-ontology-and-the-semantics-of-ātmanepada" class="blank linked">Bhartṛhari’s Linguistic Ontology and the Semantics of Ātmanepada</a>, by Dilip Loundo - Specialized study of Bhartrhari’s metaphysics of language and grammatical semantics.</p>
<p><a href="/library/the-knowledge-structure-in-the-amarakosha" class="blank linked">The Knowledge Structure in the Amarakosha</a>, by Sivaja S. Nair - Study of how the Amarakosha encodes categories, semantic relations, and systems of knowledge.</p>
<p><a href="/library/vakyapadiya" class="blank linked">Vākyapadīya</a>, by Bhartṛhari - Foundational work of Bhartrhari on language, meaning, cognition, and the philosophy of linguistic unity.</p>
<p><a href="/library/amarakosha-english-translation" class="blank linked">Amarakosha English Translation</a>, by NG Sardesai - English translation of the Amarakosha, the classical Sanskrit lexicon arranged by semantic groupings.</p>
<p><a href="/library/ideas-of-history-in-sanskrit-literature" class="blank linked">Ideas of History in Sanskrit Literature</a>, by RC Majumdar - Study of how historical consciousness appears in Sanskrit narrative, genealogy, and literary memory.</p>
<p><a href="/library/illustrated-amarakosha-in-4-languages" class="blank linked">Illustrated Amarakosha in 4 Languages</a>, by Dr. H Madhav - Multilingual and illustrated presentation of the Amarakosha for comparative and pedagogical use.</p>
<p><a href="/library/introduction-to-sanskrit-shabdamitra" class="blank linked">Introduction to Sanskrit Shabdamitra</a>, by Kulkarni, Joshi et al - Introductory Sanskrit learning aid focused on vocabulary, forms, and basic usage.</p>
<p><a href="/library/a-practical-sanskrit-introductory" class="blank linked">A Practical Sanskrit Introductory</a>, by C Wikner - Beginner-oriented introduction to Sanskrit language and basic grammar.</p>
<p><a href="/library/sanskrit-grammar" class="blank linked">Sanskrit Grammar</a>, by WD Whitney - Reference-style overview of Sanskrit grammar, inflection, and syntax.</p>
