---
title: Darshanas and Philosophy
description: Works on the major schools of Indian philosophy, including Nyaya, Vaisheshika, Sankhya, Yoga, Mimamsa, Vedanta, and Shaiva thought.
---

<p><a href="/library/a-primer-of-indian-logic" class="blank linked">A Primer of Indian Logic</a>, by S. Kuppuswami Sastri - Introductory exposition of Indian logical method, categories, inference, and debate using the Tarkasangraha tradition.</p>
<p><a href="/library/an-elementary-treatise-on-mimamsa" class="blank linked">An Elementary Treatise on Mimamsa</a>, by Laugakshi Bhaskara - Accessible introduction to Mimamsa principles, Vedic injunctions, and hermeneutical reasoning.</p>
<p><a href="/library/gangesas-theory-of-truth" class="blank linked">Gangesa's Theory of Truth</a>, by Jitendra Nath Mohanty - Study of Navya-Nyaya truth theory and the epistemic conditions of valid cognition.</p>
<p><a href="/library/gaudapada-karika" class="blank linked">Gaudapada Karika</a>, by Gaudapada - Classical Advaita work on non-duality, consciousness, and the Mandukya Upanishad.</p>
<p><a href="/library/gautamas-nyayasutras" class="blank linked">Gautama's Nyayasutras</a>, by Gautama - Edition or translation of the foundational Nyaya sutras on knowledge, inference, debate, and liberation.</p>
<p><a href="/library/gheranda-samhita" class="blank linked">Gheranda Samhita</a>, by Translated by Srisa Chandra Vasu - Hatha yoga manual on bodily discipline, purification, mudra, meditation, and yogic accomplishment.</p>
<p><a href="/library/hindu-realism-an-introduction-to-vaisheshika" class="blank linked">Hindu Realism An Introduction to Vaisheshika</a>, by JC Chatterjee - Introductory study of Vaisheshika realism, categories of being, and metaphysical analysis.</p>
<p><a href="/library/outline-of-hindu-philosophy" class="blank linked">Outline of Hindu Philosophy</a>, by M. Hiriyanna - Broad survey of major schools and recurrent philosophical themes in Hindu thought.</p>
<p><a href="/library/paratrishika-vivarana-by-abhinavagupta" class="blank linked">Paratrishika Vivarana by Abhinavagupta</a>, by Jaideva Singh - Abhinavagupta’s esoteric Shaiva commentary on language, mantra, consciousness, and manifestation.</p>
<p><a href="/library/patanjalis-yogasutras" class="blank linked">Patanjali's Yogasutras</a>, by Patanjali - Edition or translation of the Yoga Sutras with emphasis on mind, practice, samadhi, and liberation.</p>
<p><a href="/library/purva-mimamsa-in-its-sources" class="blank linked">Purva Mimamsa in its Sources</a>, by Dr. Ganganatha Jha - Source-based introduction to Purva Mimamsa doctrine and textual method.</p>
<p><a href="/library/quintessence-of-brahmasutra" class="blank linked">Quintessence of Brahmasutra</a>, by Mahamahopadhyaya Iranganti Rangacharya - Introductory digest of Vedanta centered on the Brahmasutra tradition and its key metaphysical claims.</p>
<p><a href="/library/sankhya-philosophy-by-banerji" class="blank linked">Sankhya Philosophy by Banerji</a>, by Chandra Banerji - Interpretive exposition of the Sankhya school and its key doctrines.</p>
<p><a href="/library/sastradipika" class="blank linked">Sastradipika</a>, by Parthasarathi Mishra, translated by D. Venkatramiah - Classical Mimamsa text on interpretation, injunction, and the structure of Vedic authority.</p>
<p><a href="/library/siddhantabindu" class="blank linked">Siddhantabindu</a>, by Srimat Madhusudana Sarasvati, translated by Achalananda - Vedantic sub-commentarial text presenting core Advaita positions in a compact form.</p>
<p><a href="/library/six-ways-of-knowing" class="blank linked">Six Ways of Knowing</a>, by D.M. Datta - Scholarly work on Indian thought and civilization with relevance to the themes signaled by its title.</p>
<p><a href="/library/the-nyaya-theory-of-knowledge" class="blank linked">The Nyaya Theory of Knowledge</a>, by S.C. Chatterjee - Study of Nyaya epistemology with emphasis on pramanas, error, and valid cognition.</p>
<p><a href="/library/the-paramarthasara-of-adisesa" class="blank linked">The Paramarthasara of Adisesa</a>, by Adi Sesa - Philosophical text associated with non-dual Shaiva traditions and the path to liberation.</p>
<p><a href="/library/the-purva-mimamsa-sutras-of-jaimini" class="blank linked">The Purva Mimamsa Sutras of Jaimini</a>, by Jaimini, translated by Dr. Ganganatha Jha - Foundational Mimamsa sutra text on ritual duty, injunction, and scriptural interpretation.</p>
<p><a href="/library/the-sankhya-aphorisms-of-kapila" class="blank linked">The Sankhya Aphorisms of Kapila</a>, by Kapila, translated by J. Ballantyne - Presentation of the Sankhya aphorism tradition on purusha, prakriti, tattvas, and liberation.</p>
<p><a href="/library/the-sankhya-karika-of-iswara-krishna" class="blank linked">The Sankhya Karika of Iswara Krishna</a>, by HT Colebrooke - Classical Sankhya summary text on ontology, causation, bondage, and release.</p>
<p><a href="/library/the-sankhya-philosophy" class="blank linked">The Sankhya Philosophy</a>, by Major BD Basu - Translation or study of Sankhya metaphysics, dualism, and liberation theory.</p>
<p><a href="/library/the-sankhya-system-by-keith" class="blank linked">The Sankhya System by Keith</a>, by AB Keith - Scholarly account of Sankhya doctrine in historical and comparative perspective.</p>
<p><a href="/library/the-vaisheshika-darshan" class="blank linked">The Vaisheshika Darshan</a>, by John Wells - Presentation of the Vaisheshika system and its ontology of substances, qualities, and relations.</p>
<p><a href="/library/the-yogasutras-of-patanjali" class="blank linked">The Yogasutras of Patanjali</a>, by Patanjali, translated by Sriram Lele - Translation and explanation of Patanjali’s Yoga Sutras focused on discipline, concentration, and liberation.</p>
<p><a href="/library/yogasutra-with-commentary-of-madhavacharya" class="blank linked">Yogasutra with Commentary of Madhavacharya</a>, by Madhvacharya - Yoga Sutra text accompanied by traditional commentary for philosophical and practical interpretation.</p>
<p><a href="/library/64-arts-in-ancient-india" class="blank linked">64 Arts in Ancient India</a>, by Anil Baran Ganguly - Survey of the classical list of sixty-four arts and what it reveals about education and cultured accomplishment.</p>
<p><a href="/library/amarakosha-devanagari" class="blank linked">Amarakośaḥ Devanāgarī</a>, by Amarasiṃha - Devanagari text of the Amarakosha for direct study of the classical Sanskrit lexicon.</p>
<p><a href="/library/introduction-to-iks" class="blank linked">Introduction to IKS</a>, by Kapil Kapoor - Survey introduction to Indian Knowledge Systems and their major domains, methods, and categories.</p>
<p><a href="/library/language-and-reality-world-view-of-the-nyaya-vaisesika-system-of-indian-philosophy" class="blank linked">Language and Reality, World View of the Nyaya-Vaisesika System of Indian Philosophy</a>, by VN Jha - Study of how Nyaya-Vaisheshika links language, ontology, and realism.</p>
<p><a href="/library/essays-on-tradition-recovery-and-freedom" class="blank linked">Essays on Tradition, Recovery and Freedom</a>, by Dharampal - Essays on cultural continuity, intellectual decolonization, and the recovery of civilizational freedom.</p>
<p><a href="/library/hermeneutics-and-narrative-architecture" class="blank linked">Hermeneutics and Narrative Architecture</a>, by Vishwa Adluri - Study of how interpretive method and narrative design work together in Sanskrit epic literature.</p>
<p><a href="/library/the-crisis-of-hinduism" class="blank linked">The Crisis of Hinduism</a>, by AK Saran - Reflection on modern challenges facing Hindu civilizational confidence, continuity, and self-understanding.</p>
<p><a href="/library/the-perils-of-textual-transmission" class="blank linked">The Perils of Textual Transmission</a>, by Vishwa Adluri - Scholarly work on Indian thought and civilization with relevance to the themes signaled by its title.</p>
<p><a href="/library/the-redemption-of-the-brahman" class="blank linked">The Redemption of the Brahman</a>, by Vishwa Adluri, Joydeep Bagchee - Interpretive essay on the figure of the Brahman and debates about textual and cultural transformation.</p>
<p><a href="/library/a-study-of-tarakasamgraha-as-compromise-between-nyaya-and-vaisheshika" class="blank linked">A Study of Tarakasamgraha as Compromise Between Nyaya and Vaisheshika</a>, by Priya M. P. - Detailed study of the Tarkasamgraha as a bridge text combining Nyaya logic with Vaisheshika ontology.</p>
<p><a href="/library/abhinavagupta-a-historical-and-philosophical-study" class="blank linked">Abhinavagupta a Historical and Philosophical Study</a>, by Unknown - Study of Abhinavagupta’s life, thought, Kashmir Shaivism, and aesthetic philosophy.</p>
<p><a href="/library/the-mimamsa-sutras-of-jaimini" class="blank linked">The Mimamsa Sutras of Jaimini</a>, by Jaimini - Another edition of the Mimamsa Sutras presenting the core framework of Vedic hermeneutics and duty.</p>
